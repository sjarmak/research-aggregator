*Academic Research*

*<https://ui.adsabs.harvard.edu/abs/2025arXiv251105385Z/abstract|TeaRAG: A Token-Efficient Agentic Retrieval-Augmented Generation Framework>*
*Source:* Zhang et al.
*Why it matters:* TeaRAG optimizes retrieval and reasoning processes in RAG, directly addressing context management and retrieval efficiency.
*Key Takeaways:*
• Utilizes chunk-based semantic retrieval with graph retrieval using concise triplets.
• Implements Iterative Process-aware Direct Preference Optimization (IP-DPO) to reduce reasoning steps.
• Achieves significant token reduction while improving Exact Match scores on multiple datasets.

TeaRAG introduces a framework that compresses retrieval content and reasoning steps, enhancing the efficiency of RAG systems by reducing token overhead and improving accuracy.

*<https://ui.adsabs.harvard.edu/abs/2025arXiv251106179W/abstract|MemoriesDB: A Temporal-Semantic-Relational Database for Long-Term Agent Memory>*
*Source:* Ward, Joel
*Why it matters:* MemoriesDB discusses a database architecture for long-term memory and retrieval, crucial for agent memory systems.
*Key Takeaways:*
• Combines time-series, vector database, and graph system properties.
• Supports efficient time-bounded retrieval and hybrid semantic search.
• Demonstrates scalable recall and contextual reinforcement using standard relational infrastructure.

MemoriesDB offers a unified architecture to maintain coherence across time, meaning, and relation, enhancing long-term memory capabilities in AI systems.

*<https://ui.adsabs.harvard.edu/abs/2025arXiv251101643C/abstract|A Graph-based RAG for Energy Efficiency Question Answering>*
*Source:* Campi et al.
*Why it matters:* This study explores a graph-based RAG for question answering, directly related to retrieval and context management.
*Key Takeaways:*
• Automatically extracts a Knowledge Graph from energy field documents.
• Provides accurate multilingual answers with a 75.2% accuracy rate.
• Demonstrates promising multilingual abilities with minimal accuracy loss due to translation.

This work highlights the potential of graph-based RAG architectures in providing accurate and multilingual question answering in the energy sector.

*<https://ui.adsabs.harvard.edu/abs/2025arXiv251101268K/abstract|Rescuing the Unpoisoned: Efficient Defense against Knowledge Corruption Attacks on RAG Systems>*
*Source:* Kim et al.
*Why it matters:* Directly addresses RAG and defense mechanisms, relevant to context and retrieval optimization.
*Key Takeaways:*
• Introduces RAGDefender, a resource-efficient defense mechanism against data poisoning attacks.
• Operates during the post-retrieval phase to filter out adversarial content.
• Outperforms existing defenses, significantly reducing attack success rates.

RAGDefender offers a lightweight solution to protect RAG systems from knowledge corruption, enhancing the robustness of retrieval processes.

*<https://ui.adsabs.harvard.edu/abs/2025arXiv251104720K/abstract|Learning to reason about rare diseases through retrieval-augmented agents>*
*Source:* Kim et al.
*Why it matters:* Focuses on retrieval-augmented diagnostic reasoning, relevant to context and retrieval in AI systems.
*Key Takeaways:*
• Uses AI agents to retrieve clinically relevant evidence for rare disease detection.
• Integrates with diverse LLMs to improve pathology recognition and interpretability.
• Achieves up to a 10.2% performance gain on rare disease datasets.

RADAR demonstrates the power of retrieval-augmented reasoning in improving diagnostic accuracy for rare diseases in medical imaging.
